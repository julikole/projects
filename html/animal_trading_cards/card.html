Quokka

Dutch captain Willem de Vlanmigh named the island ‘t Eylandt ‘t Rottenest (Rat’s Nest Island) in 1696, mistaking the quokkas for giant rats.

  • Sientific Name: Setonix brachyurus
  • Average Height: 50 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 10 years
  • Habitat: From semiarid scrub to cultivated gardens

Quokkas store fat in their tails as a means of surviving when food supplies are low, and they can also go months without water. They swallow food whole, then regurgitate it and eat it again, which allows them to absorb the nutrients of their food better.
